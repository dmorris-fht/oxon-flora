### *Epipogium aphyllum* Ghost Orchid 
Sw.
#### Ecology
This tiny, much searched for, orchid of leaf-litter in dark beech woods is completely dependent on fungi, notably Tricholoma cingulatum; and slug damage can destroy its few, sporadic flowers.
#### Status & distribution
Always restricted to a few localities in Herefordshire, eastern Wales and the  Oxfordshire Chilterns. In a small area of the Chilterns it was reported but not coroborated at c. six sites.
#### Trends
Three confirmed sightings in the Chilterns  last confirmed sighting in 1979, or 1976, by J E Lousley (S R Cole 2014).
#### Conservaton work
Slug pellets have been put out and search parties organized. The plant cannot be grown in isolation as it relies on fungi to obtain nutrients from trees of various species, mostly beech.

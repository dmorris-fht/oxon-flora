### *Erica cinerea* Bell Heather 
L.
#### Ecology
This dwarf shrub occurs in open heaths on well-drained acidic soils with very low nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
Throughout the British Isles except south central England. In Oxfordshire confined to the Corallian Ridge and the gravels of the Chilterns plateau.
#### Trends
In southeast England this species has declined dramatically, especially over chalk. Probably lost since c. 2008 in the Oxfordshire Cotswolds (Tadmarton and Wychwood), and on the Corallian Ridge (Buckland Warren and Boars Hill), Of the c. 11 sites known in the Chilterns, six have been seen during 2000-22.
#### Conservaton work
Easily shaded out by taller vegetation. Re-introduced from nursery stock on Boars HIll, and thriving; introduced on Kingwood Common where it requires control of young trees.

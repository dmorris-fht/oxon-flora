### *Cephalanthera damasonium* White Helleborine 
(Mill.) Druce
#### Ecology
This rhizomatous perennial is a strict calcicole which flourishes with raised nutrients. It grows in both open and shady conditions.
#### Status & distribution
Confined to the limestone and chalk areas of southern England. In Oxfordshire widely distributed on lime-rich soils, but especially frequent in the Chilterns.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) listed 27 localities. Recorded in 52 tetrads by Killick et al. (1998) in vc 23, and at c. 56 sites the county and 49 in vc 23 in 2000-22, suggesting it has not declined markedly. Also it appears quite often in urban and new sites.
#### Conservaton work
None.

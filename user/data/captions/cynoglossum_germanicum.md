### *Cynoglossum germanicum* Green Hound's-tongue 
Jacq.
#### Ecology
This is an inconspicuous biennial or short-lived perennial that grows on moist but well-drained calcareous soils in woods and woodland edges. It appeared in abundance in Surrey after the 1987 storms, indicating long survival of seed in the soil. It has hooked seeds and tolerates high nutrients, explaining its presence along old drove roads.
#### Status & distribution
Once widespread, but never common, in Gloucestershire, Oxfordshire and Surrey. In Oxfordshire lost at one site that became infested with cleavers, probably following nitrogen runoff from the adjacent field.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) had six sites, including Pyrton, where it is now spreading, and two sites near Woodstock, perhaps Bladon Quarry where R C Palmer found it in 1970, though searching with him in 1994 C R Lambrick could not find it. A population introduced at Warburg Reserve  in 1991 reached many hundreds in 2015 and is still spreading.
#### Conservaton work
The Oxfordshire Flora Group, under NE, has been monitoring and managing the two sites since 1998, and has found that the fluctuating populations respond well to reduction of shading, and removal of ivy and cleavers. Garden experiments showed that it is self-fertile, and germinates and establishes readily but is easily shaded out. Seed (from Knightsbridge Lane, Pyrton) sown by Judy Webb in 2015 in the triangle formed by disused road just outside Aston Rowant NNR. Thirty three plants flowered well in 2017  and produced c. 300 rosettes in 2018. More was scattered along Hill Lane in 2018.

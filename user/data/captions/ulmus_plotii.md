### *Ulmus plotii* Plot's Elm 
Druce
#### Ecology
This distinctive narrow tree with small, narrow leaves was first described from Hanwell near Banbury by Robert Plot (1677). It formerly lent a characteristic appearance to the landscape of northeast England where it was most abundant, but has declined. It is usually found in hedgerows on neutral to base-rich, deep soils along river valleys. At Hanwell it was both planted and “wild” but has been lost most likely to Dutch Elm disease, to which it is very susceptible. R C Palmer recorded it in 1974 on the  east side of the River Ray in hedge at bottom of 1st field below bridge southeast of Astleybridge Farm, Merton. DNA studies indicate that only material from the same clone has all the characteristic features of the species (Coleman et al. 2000), while morphologically similar plants are genetically dispersed in the U. minor group.
#### Trends
The type locality at Hanwell does not seem to have any surviving.

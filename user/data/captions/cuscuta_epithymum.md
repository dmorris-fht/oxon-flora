### *Cuscuta epithymum* Dodder 
(L.) L.
#### Ecology
This parasitic annual (rarely perennial) grows on the stems of small shrubs and herbs; mostly on Calluna vulgaris, Thymus polytrichoides, Ulex gallii and U. minor according to the New Atlas (Preston et al. 2002), and on a wide range of hosts especially on legumes (as at Holly Hill) according to Stace (2010). Kew website: When growing on perennial hosts, Cuscuta epithymum induces the formation of galls (growths of host tissue) where parasitic tissue is able to overwinter. The following spring, new clover dodder plants develop from these galls. It's seed is long-lived in the soil (BSBI website.
#### Status & distribution
Widespread in the British isles, but common only in the south. In Oxfordshire mostly on the chalk.
#### Trends
In vc 23 Druce (1927) reported it at six sites in heathland on gorse, heathers and ivy, and it was found near Charlbury till 1935, but Killick et al. (1998) recorded six tetrads almost all on chalk grassland in the Chilterns on Lotus, Genista, Knautia, Euphrasia and Pimpinella. It now seems to be mainly associated with Origanum and Thymus on and around anthills; at Elmorepark Woods and with Galium verum and grasses at Straw Hill.

None.

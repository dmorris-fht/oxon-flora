### *Agrostis curtisii* Bristle Bent 
Kerguélen
#### Ecology
This small, tufted, perennial grass grows in extremely nutrient poor, acidic, sandy soils of heaths and open woodland.
#### Status & distribution
Common in the southwest of England and Wales, but absent from Scotland and Ireland. In Oxfordshire it is at the northeast edge of its range. Probably previously overlooked at Tadmarton Heath, and possibly introduced at Sydlings Copse by machinery from Berkshire.
#### Trends
Reported by Sibthorp (1794) (as A. mutabilis) at two sites, Druce (1927) said that three sites were probably errors. Not mentioned in Killick et al. (1998). Possibly spreading or, more likely, better recorded, as it was only since 2000 that it was discovered at  two other sites in Oxfordshire which extend its known native distribution.

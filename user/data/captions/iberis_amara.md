### *Iberis amara* Wild Candytuft 
L.
#### Ecology
This is an annual, a calcicole which favours bare open ground and low nutrients. It has declined since myxomatosis, as the plant may have been dependent on rabbits to provide suitable areas for germination. It has a long-lived seed bank as it regenerates in old sites after disturbance.
#### Status & distribution
Mostly on the outcrops of chalk from Swindon to Cambridge. In Oxfordshire on the Chilterns, especially the scarp slopes, three records on the Wessex Downs and one near Banbury.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) listed c. 41 sites, Killick et al. (1998) 28 tetads, and in  2000-22 c. 12  localities in Oxfordshire and nine in vc23.
#### Conservaton work
Scrapes put in at Aston Upthorpe for the juniper might prove beneficial for candytuft.

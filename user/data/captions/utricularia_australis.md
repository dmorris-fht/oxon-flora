### *Utricularia australis* Bladderwort 
R.Br.
#### Ecology
This carnivorous submerged aquatic favours acidic and low nutrient water, but also tolerates some lime.
#### Status & distribution
It is widespread but uncommon throughout the British Isles; records are more frequent from the south, perhaps because identification depends on flowering, and even then is difficult. It favours neutral conditions with low nutrients. In Oxfordshire mostly on the Corallian Ridge.
#### Trends
Druce listed two sites in vc23 as well as its stronghold at Cothill. Killick et al. (1998) cited Otmoor, but in 2000-22 also in the Chilterns again.
#### Conservaton work
Extensive work at the Ruskin Reserve, Cothill is restoring its open water habitat.
#### Variation
Identification depends on flowering, and even then is difficult.

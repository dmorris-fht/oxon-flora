### *Pyrola minor* Common Wintergreen 
L.
#### Ecology
This rhizomatous, mycorrhizal, evergreen herb grows in deep leaf litter in boggy woodlands and plantations which are acidic and very low in nutrients.  There is evidence from related species that Pyrola spp. utilise one fungus after germination and another when mature (Hashimoto at al. 2012).
#### Status & distribution
Scattered throughout the British Isles, but mostly in the north. In Oxfordshire probably completely confined to the Chilterns.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) reported 20 sites, all in the Chilterns, Killick et al. (1998)  four tetrads, including an unconfirmed record at Sarsgrove Wood in the north Cotswolds.

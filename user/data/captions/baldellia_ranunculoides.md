### *Baldellia ranunculoides* Lesser Water-plantain 
(L.) Parl.
#### Ecology
This attractive aquatic is usually rooted in the drawdown zone of still or slowly moving water, tolerating a range of alkalinity, very low nutrients and some salinity.
#### Status & distribution
Scattered in the British Isles, but moslty in central west Ireland, and hotspots notably  Angelsey, the Broads and the New Forest. It used to be rare  but widespread in Oxfordshire.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) listed seven sites. Killick et al (1998) reported five sites, and it has been seen during 2000-22 at five areas all within Otmoor, more or less 500 m from each other.
#### Conservaton work
The Freshwater Habitats Trust introduced it at Pinkhill, Farmoor (source Fowls Pill) in 2017. Present every year up to last survey in August 2022 when c. ten plants were found.

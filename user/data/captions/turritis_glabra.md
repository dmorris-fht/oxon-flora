### *Turritis glabra* Tower Cress 
L.
#### Ecology
This small crucifer is a biennial or short-lived perennial. It is usually found on disturbed or open grassy, dry, slightly calcareous banks with raised nutrients. Seeds can survive for long periods in the soil.
#### Status & distribution
Mostly in southeast England, it has strongholds in the Breckland, Worcestershire and Hampshire. Scattered in Oxfordshire.
#### Trends
Nationally this species has declined hugely. Druce (1927) had four sites, Killick et al. (1998) three tetrads. None in 2000-22.
#### Conservaton work
None.

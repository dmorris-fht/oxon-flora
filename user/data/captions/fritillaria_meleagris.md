### *Fritillaria meleagris* Fritillary 
L.
#### Ecology
This iconic bulbous perennial flourishes in damp or winter flooded hay meadows, preferring alkaline conditions and tolerating moderate nutrients. Spread mostly by seed, but seeds not long lived in the soil, and the bulbs can remain dormant https://besjournals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/1365-2745.13886
#### Status & distribution
Widespread in central and southern England, scattered elsewhere in the British Isles.
#### Trends
Increasing and often planted and naturalizing readily. Druce (1927) pointed out that it was not recorded in the wild around Oxford till 1735 when it was included in C B P Blackstone's list of Harefield plants. Both Druce and Killick at al. (1998) reported about 20 sites for it. In 2000-22 in about 23 wild sites. It recently (2011) appeared in West Mead.
#### Conservaton work
At Iffley Meadows it responded very well to resumption of the haycut and aftermath grazing regime restarted by BBOWT. At North Hinksey it was set back by April flooding, as noted in the Biological Flora (Tatarenko et al. 2022) https://besjournals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/1365-2745.13886

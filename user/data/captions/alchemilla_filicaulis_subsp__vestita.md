### *Alchemilla filicaulis subsp. vestita* Hairy Lady's-mantle 
(Buser) M.E.Bradshaw
#### Ecology
This is a perennial apomict which is found in rough pasture and woodland borders. It favours neutral soils with low nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
Widespread in the British Isles, especially the north and west. Druce recognized two varieties of Alchemilla  - A. vulgaris var. minor (= A. filicaulis Buser) for which he gave c. 31 localities in vc 23 and Bagley in vc22; and A. vulgaris var. vulgaris (= A. pratensis Schmidt = A. xanthochlora) for which he gave two localities - Kiddington and Pyrton Scrubs; this taxon was last recorded in 1988 in the county. Killick et al. (1998) recorded A. filicaulis subsp. vestita in 40 tetrads mostly in the northwest of vc 23. During 2000-22, it has been recorded at only six localities in the county.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) listed 31 sites and Killick et al. (1998) 40 tetrads. During 2000-22 this subspecies has been recorded at only 10 sites in Oxfordshire of which eight are in vc 23.
#### Conservaton work
At Ashdown House (in the care of the National Trust) a change in the mowing regime of the Broad Ride resulted in an increase in area and number of plants recorded.

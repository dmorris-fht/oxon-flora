### *Sium latifolium* Greater Water-parsnip 
L.
#### Ecology
This large perennial grows on the banks of streams and rivers, prefering neutral to calcareous conditions and tolerates raised nutrients. It is susceptible to grazing and shading.
#### Status & distribution
Mostly in southeastern England with a stronghold on the Somerset Levels. In Oxfordshire largely along the main rivers.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) listed 19 sites, as did Killick et al. (1998) but probably an overestimate. In 2000-22 at two sites.
#### Conservaton work
Fencing has been provided by Farm Animal Initiatives at the Wytham site to control grazing. Seed has been collected from the Wytham population, grown on by the Oxford Botanic Garden and used to re-enforce the population, and to establish populations at Millham Ford, Oxford and  at Pinkhill, Farmoor, by Eynsham (planted by Judy Webb 2018), but it didn't survive after 2019.

### *Bromopsis benekenii* Lesser Hairy-brome 
(Lange) Holub
#### Ecology
This tufted woodland perennial favours humus-rich alkaline soils, and tolerates moderate nutrient levels.
#### Status & distribution
Thinnly scattered from Kent to the central Highlands of Scotland,  with a stronghold in the Chilterns. It was formerly under-recorded perhaps due to its similarity to B. ramosa, with which it often grows. In Oxfordshire restricted to the Chilterns except for one record from the Cotswolds.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) recognized this taxon as a variety of B. ramosa and only gave one locality (Mapledurham with no date or recorder). Killick et al. (1998) listed 11 sites. During 2000-20 at three sites, one not refound.
#### Conservaton work
None.
#### Variation
An intermediate was reported at Nettlebed SP7084 in 1978 by D. Mabberley & A Sing (Killick et al. 1998 p. 282)

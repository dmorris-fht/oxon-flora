### *Oenanthe silaifolia* Narrow-leaved Water-dropwort 
M.Bieb.
#### Ecology
This perennial umbellifer of floodplain hay meadows prefers slightly alkaline conditions and slightly raised nutrients. At West Mead (Oxford Meadows SAC) it tends to grow with Meadow Rue Thalictrum flavum along the palaeochannels. At Bestmoor SSSI O. silaifolia occurs as the only common forb in a grass-rich floodplain meadow, suggesting it may be herbicide resistant.
#### Status & distribution
Locally scattered in southern England and southeat Wales, but absent from Scotland, Ireland and northern and south-west England. In Oxofrdshire in the River Ray catchment and one site on Pixey and Yarnton Meadows.
#### Trends
Still present where Druce found it on the Cherwell and Oxford Meadows, ten sites. New sites on the River Ray are being found, probably reflecting increased recording, its early flowering and difficulty of vegetative identification. Killick et al. (1998) had 12 tetrads in vc 23 and during 2000-22 about 13.
#### Conservaton work
It is abundant at Meadow Farm, Blackthorn, BBOWT reserve, perhaps helped by its tolerance of moderate nitrogen levels.

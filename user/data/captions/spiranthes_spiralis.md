### *Spiranthes spiralis* Autumn Lady's-tresses 
(L.) Chevall.
#### Ecology
This species is confined to short sward on shallow, nutrient poor, calcareous or acidic soils. One site is grazed by horses, Hackpen by cattle with rabbits, and at Kingwood this plant grows with heather on top of a covered reservoir managed by cutting.
#### Status & distribution
Mostly a plant of southern England and coastal in Ireland and Wales. In Oxfordshire mostly in the Chilterns, but also on the Cotswold limestone and the Corallian Ridge.
#### Trends
It declined mostly before 1960. Druce (1927) listed 14 sites and Killick et al. (1998) only three. In 2000-22 four localities in Oxfordshire and three in vc 23..
#### Conservaton work
No specific measures.

### *Hydrocharis morsus-ranae* Frogbit 
L.
#### Ecology
This free-floating plant of still water likes neutral to base-rich conditions and tolerates quite high nutrients. It  sets seed rarely, and spreads by vegetative buds.
#### Status & distribution
It is widespread in central, southern and western England and Wales, and central Ireland. In Oxfordshire mainly in the River Ray catchment and on the Chilterns plateau.
#### Trends
It was in 20 Druce (1927) sites but only four tetrads in Killick et al. (1998). Now surviving only at Otmoor where it has also colonized the New Pill made by the RSPB and EA.
#### Conservaton work
In cultivation (source Fowls Pill) under the Freshwater Habitats Trust GroWet programme. Introduced  at Pinkhill, Farmoor in 2018, spreading to a large population of many 1000s of plants in 2022, also to new pools. It is flourishing in the areas restored on Otmoor by the RSPB.

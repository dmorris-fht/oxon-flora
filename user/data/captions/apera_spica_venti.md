### *Apera spica-venti* Loose Silky-bent 
(L.) P.Beauv.
#### Ecology
This elegant annual is usually transient in arable or waste places, on light dry soils  with neutral pH and moderate nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
Scattered in England with hotspots on the Surrey heaths and in the Breckland. In Oxfordshire it is found mainly along the Corallian Ridge.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) reported five sites and Killick et al. (1998) 12 tetrads in vc 23. During 2000-22 reported at five localities, two of them in vc 23.
#### Conservaton work
In order to look for seed bank species at Dry Sandford Pit in 2002 M J Crawley had a rabbit-proof fence put up and the ground inside cultivated. About ten A. spica-venti came up and flowered that year and again in 2004, but not subsequently. "The cultivation treatment was not repeated, so there were no more opportunities for recruitment of annuals. It would be good to repeat the experiment  with more replication."

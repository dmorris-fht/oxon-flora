### *Blitum bonus-henricus* Good-King-Henry 
(L.)Rchb.
#### Ecology
This perennial is well known from prehistoric records and was cultivated as a green vegetable. It grows in very high-nutrient waste places. Braithwaite et al. (2006) found a significant decrease in 1987/8 - 2003/4. It was often on roadsides by farms, which are now less enriched by dung.
#### Status & distribution
Widespread in England, Wales and southeast Scotland. In Oxfordshire widespread but mostly in the north and northwest.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) listed 35 sites, Killick et al. (998) reported 106 tetrads. Braithwaite et al. (2006) found a significant decrease in 1987/8 - 2003/4. Only 18 localities have been recorded in 2000-22.
#### Conservaton work
None.

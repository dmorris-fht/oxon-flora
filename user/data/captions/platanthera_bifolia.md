### *Platanthera bifolia* Lesser Butterfly-orchid 
(L.) Rich.
#### Ecology
This species is remarkably tolerant of soil conditions, being found in acid bogs and calcareous fens as well as in drier woodland and grassland habitats which have low nutrient levels.
#### Status & distribution
Scattered throughout the British Isles, but commonest in the north and west. Scattered in Oxfordshire, but mainly on the Chilterns plateau.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) cited this species as rare with 19 localities, and Killick et al. (1998) gave 16 tetrads. There are only four 2000-22 records - two major populations, one in Wychwood forest SSSI in the west and Warburg Reserve SSSI BBOWT  in the south; also on the Corallian Ridge.
#### Conservaton work
Detailed monitoring at the largest site was begum by Terry Swainbank and is being continued.

### *Blechnum spicant* Hard-fern 
(L.) Roth
#### Ecology
A small evergreen fern of shady places and ditch banks on lime-free, acid soils with low nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
Common in Scotland, Ireland, Wales and northwest and southern England, but rare in the central and east Midlands. In Oxfordshire widespread over the county except for the clay vales.
#### Trends
By 1968 eight of Druce's 11 sites could no longer be found, and Killick et al. (1998) reported four extant sites. During 2000-22 six localities have been recorded in Oxfordshire vc 23,  and five in vc23, two of the five new ones being in parks where this fern may have been introduced (Buscot Park and Nuneham Courtnay).

### *Aira caryophyllea* Silver Hair-grass 
L.
#### Ecology
A small winter annual of dry open places on free draining, mostly sandy, soils. It is found in neutral to slightly acidic conditions with very low nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
Widespread and common especially in coastal and upland areas of the British Isles, rare in the Scottish highlands and agricultural areas of the south-east Midlands. Mostly in the north of Oxfordshire, along the Corallian sands and on the gravels of the Chilterns plateau.
#### Trends
Recorded numbers fluctuate greatly, and as it flowers early, it is often not easy to detect later in the season. Druce (1927) reported 22 sites, and it was recorded in 37 tetrads by Killick et al. (1998), most seemingly now lost. 0f these 21 were north of Northings 30 and at one of four sites re-visited it was re-found. As the other 17 northerly sites were not re-visited, the species may not be rare, unless scrub growth has eliminated former railway sites. In 2000-22 it was recorded in eight sites in Oxfordshire, and three in vc 23.
#### Conservaton work
None. Open conditions need to be maintained.

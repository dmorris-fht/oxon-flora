### *Campanula latifolia* Giant Bellflower 
L.
#### Ecology
This large perennial herb thrives in deep soil in damp woods, preferring slightly alkaline conditions and raised nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
Mostly in the north and west of England, and the south and east of Scotland. Often introduced as an ornamental. It was possibly native in the Cotswolds and the Chilterns though not recorded until 1906.
#### Trends
Recorded as native by Druce (1927) at one site and by Killick et al. (1998) at 16 tetrads, During 2000-22 at two sites in vc 23.
#### Conservaton work
None.

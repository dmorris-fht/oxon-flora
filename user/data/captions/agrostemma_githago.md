### *Agrostemma githago* Corncockle 
L.
#### Ecology
A handsome annual of cereal crops and waste places.  On many soil types, preferring neutral pH and moderate nutrient levels.
#### Status & distribution
Introduced as a grain contaminant in the Iron Age. Widespread in central and southern England, occasional Wales, Ireland and southern and eastern Scotland. Scattered in Oxfordshire, but least on the Wessex Downs and the clay vales.
#### Trends
Sibthorp (1794) gave no localiites, suggesting it was common, Druce (1927) gave 48 localities and Killick et al. (1998) listed 11 tetrads since 1941, but some of these were from gardens. Last wild in c. 1992, Now frequent due to sowing of wildflower mixes.
#### Conservaton work
Many headlands and urban spaces are now sown with wildflower mixes which include this dramatic plant.
#### Variation
Druce recorded that "var. microcalyx Doell Fl. Baden,1232, with the calyx segments shorter than the flower have been noticed near South Stoke."

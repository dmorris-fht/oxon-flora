### *Epipactis phyllanthes* Green-flowered Helleborine 
G.E.Sm.
#### Ecology
This rhizomatous partial mycoheterotroph is restricted to shady or damp beech, oak or alder woods with little ground cover, preferring calcareous to neutral, mesotrophic soils.
#### Status & distribution
Widespread in the British Isles but mostly in south-central England. In Oxfordshire it is rare on the Corallian Ridge and scattered in the Chilterns.
#### Trends
Not recorded by Druce (1927), but Killick et al. (1998) reported it in nine tetrads. During 2000-22 listed in eight localities all in vc23, and all, bar Sydlings Copse, in the Chilterns.
#### Conservaton work
Always self-pollinated, pollination occurring before the flower opens.
#### Variation
Terry Swainbank has assesses the populations at Lambridge Wood and concluded they are all subspecies pendula.

### *Oreopteris limbosperma* Lemon-scented Fern 
(All.) Holub
#### Ecology
This fern of shady, damp places by streams, is common in the north and west, but has declined in the South. It prefers acidic and very low nutrient soils.
#### Status & distribution
It is common in Scotland, Wales and northern England, bust ver rare in the central and southern England, but with stronghold in the New Forest and Sussex/Kent. In Oxfordshire on lime-rich soils.
#### Trends
Known to Druce (1927) at two sites, and to Killick et al. (1998) at three tetrads, and in the Vale of White Horse at four sites on the Corallian Ridge. During 2000-22 no confirmed records.
#### Conservaton work
" Existed a year or two ago in Bagley Wood and in woods near Cumnor and Wytham but collectors and cultivation have together nearly extirpated them" Boswell 1871.

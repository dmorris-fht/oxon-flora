### *Erysimum cheiranthoides* Treacle-mustard 
L.
#### Ecology
This annual of arable, garden and waste places prefers calcareous and nutrient-rich soils. This archaeophyte is sometimes regarded as an alien.
#### Status & distribution
Scattered throughout the British Isles, but common in central and southern England. Scattered in Oxfordshire, mainly in built-up aeas.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) reported 26 sites, and Killick et al. (1998) 150 tetrads. In  2000-22 in about 23 sites in Oxfordshire and 20 in vc 23.

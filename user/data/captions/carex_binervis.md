### *Carex binervis* Green-ribbed Sedge 
Sm.
#### Ecology
In a variety of habitats - shady or not,  wet or dry, on acidic soils with very low nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
Most common in the north and west, but also in south coast counties and in the Broads. In Oxfordshire once scattered, now resticted to very acidic and very low nutrient sites in the west and east.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) listed eight sites, and Killick et al. (1998) had ten tetrads. During 2000-22 only at three sites.
#### Conservaton work
Needs work on conditions at Herbert's Heath, North Leigh Heath LWS and Nettlebed Common LWS.

### *Eleocharis quinqueflora* Few-flowered Spike-rush 
(Hartmann) O.Schwarz
#### Ecology
This small perennial spike-rush is a plant of short turf, calcareous flushes, damp hay meadows and floodplain pastures. It does not tolerate raised nutrients or lack of grazing or cutting.
#### Status & distribution
It is widespread in Scotland and Wales, but has declined in England. Scattered in Oxfordshire.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) mentioned Sibthorp's record at Lye Valley (as Scirpus pauciflorus). In 2000-22 only known from the Lye Valley SSSI, north and south areas.
#### Conservaton work
Two tiny populations survive thanks to vigilance by David Steel, Anthony Roberts, and Judy Webb, and management by Oxford City Council and the Friends of the Lye Valley.

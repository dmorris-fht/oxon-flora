### *Aira praecox* Early Hair-grass 
L.
#### Ecology
This is a very small winter annual of dry open sunny places on sandy soils. It flourishes in slightly acidic and very low nutrient grasslands.
#### Status & distribution
Widespread and common in most of the British Isles except the southeast Midlands. Widespread in Oxfordshire but mostly on the Chilterns plateau, the Corallian ridge and in the north of the county.
#### Trends
Recorded numbers fluctuate. While Druce (1927) recorded 21 sites, Killick et al. (1998) had 32 tetrads. In 2000-2022 there were 14 localities in Oxfordshire of which eight are in vc 23. It was not re-found at Tadmarton Heath but the other 19 tetrads north of Oxford haven’t been checked. It has not been seen since the 1970s near Chadlington, Leafield and Freeland which were former hot-spots. Crawley (2005) reported it as commoner than A. caryophyllea.
#### Conservaton work
It is at risk from competitors and eutrophication; it has perhaps benefited from set-aside.

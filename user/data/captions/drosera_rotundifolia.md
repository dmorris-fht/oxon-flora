### *Drosera rotundifolia* Round-leaved Sundew 
L.
#### Ecology
This small rosette-forming carnivorous perennial is typically found in bogs and damp heathy ground with very low pH and extremely low nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
It is common in the north, west and south of the British Isles, but absent from the English Midlands and scattered in Norfolk.  It survived in the calcareous fen at Cothill on tussocks of Sphagnum (which lower the pH).
#### Trends
Sibthorp reported it on the north side of Shotover, but Druce said it was lost from there by 1830. In vc 22 Oxon it was known in four localities - Bagley Wood, Chilswell, Frilford and Cothill, where it was last seen by a colleague of K Porter NE in 2004/5.
#### Conservaton work
Pony grazing at Parsonage Moor seems to have resulted in the acidic Sphagnum tussock being flatterned. The habitat is also threatened by very high nitrate run off from the adjacent maize fields, which has resulted in an abundance of nitrophilous plants (especially watercress) in the water courses.

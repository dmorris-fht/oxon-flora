### *Euphorbia exigua* Dwarf Spurge 
L.
#### Ecology
This is a small annual of dry, calcareous soils with moderate nutrients. It is locally frequent on field margins, but also on open, dry grassland and disturbed habitats.
#### Status & distribution
This plant is widespread in the British Isles, but mostly in central, southrn and eastern England. In Oxfordshire formerly ubiquitous, but now mostly north of Oxford and in the Chilterns.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) reported it as common and gernerally distributed, occurring in Oxford. Killick et al. (1998) reported 319 tetrads in vc23. During 2000-22 at c. 70 sites.

### *Schoenus nigricans* Black Bog-rush 
L.
#### Ecology
This is a small tufted perennial restricted in Oxfordshire to calcareous fens with very low nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
Abundant on the north and western seabord of Scotland and Ireland, scattered in the soulth with a stronghold in the New Forest. In Oxfordshire confined to the Corallian Ridge.
#### Trends
Druce knew this plant at Sydlings Copse and Weston Fen, it was last seen at the former in 1990 and in the latter in 1983. However it has reappeared at Lye Valley and Hinksey Heights, and still occurs in vc22 at Cothill, Frilford, Lashford Lane and Dry Sandfor Pit.
#### Conservaton work
Substantial clearance of scrub and reed by volunteeers at Lye Valley and Hinksey Heights Fen has doubtless been responsible for its reappearance there in 2019. Extensive work by BBOWT at Parsonage Moor, Dry Sandford Pit and Lashford Lane has been crucial for this species.

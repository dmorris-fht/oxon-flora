### *Hyoscyamus niger* Henbane 
L.
#### Ecology
This striking biennial inhabits rough, waste and manured land such as chicken-runs, especially on dry lime-rich and sandy soils. Druce thought that plants in newly disturbed ground could be from dormant seeds and Crawley (2005) refers to seed longevity.
#### Status & distribution
Widespread in central, eastern and southern England, scattered elsewherein the British Isles.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) reported 33 sites and Killick et al (1998)  25 tetrads, and during 2000-22 nine have been reported in the county.

None.

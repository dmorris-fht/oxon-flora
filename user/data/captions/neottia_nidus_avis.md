### *Neottia nidus-avis* Bird's-nest Orchid 
(L.) Rich.
#### Ecology
This orchid normally has no chlorophyl, but is hetero-mycotrophic - gaining nutrition from fungi which are in turn dependent on trees.
#### Status & distribution
Distributed throughout temperate Eurasia; and throughout the British Isles, though commonest in the south of England. In Oxfordshire mostly on the Chilterns plateau, to a lesser extent on the Cotswolds and rare on the Corallian Ridge.
#### Trends
Known to Druce at 42 sites, half of which were on the Chilterns. Recorded by Killick at al. at 21 tetrads. In 2000-22 in 14 localities, almost all in the Chilterns.
#### Conservaton work
None.

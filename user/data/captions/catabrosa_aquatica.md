### *Catabrosa aquatica* Whorl-grass 
(L.) P.Beauv.
#### Ecology
This water-side perennial flourishes in neutral conditions, and with high nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
Curiously distributed, being commonest on the west coast of Scotland, in eastern Norfolk and southern Hampshire.
#### Trends
While it is still common in central and eastern England, it has had a big and unexplained decline – perhaps fewer wet places where cattle congregate. Druce (1927) had it at 25 sites but declining.  Although Braithwaite et al. (2006) recorded an increase between 1987/8 and 2003/4.  It has declined steeply from 50 tetrads reported in Killick et al. (1998) to only four sites in 2000-22. But it may also be under-recorded as 35 of the Killick et al. (1998) tetrads were north of Oxford; and Crawley (2005) reported nine Oxon sites. But only four during 2000-22 in Oxfordshire and four in vc 23.

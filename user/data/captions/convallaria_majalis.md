### *Convallaria majalis* Lily-of-the-valley 
L.
#### Ecology
This attractive rhizomatous perennial is frequently planted. It is widespread in England and is an indicator of ancient woodland. It favours acidic soils in the south but limestone woodlands from the Chilterns northwards.
#### Status & distribution
It is widespread in England, Wales and Scotland and historically widespread in Oxfordshire.
#### Trends
Convallaria was known to Druce (1927) at 11 sites in vc 23 and Bagley Wood and Chilswell in vc 22 Oxon.  Perhaps native at Berrick Trench, Oakley Hill and High Wood in the 1980s. All the 2000-22 records appear likely to be recent introductions. Convalaria has been lost from several woods that still exist (eg Wychwood,  Aston Rowant, Tackley, Nettlebed, Chilswell, Bagley) the reasons are not apparent.

### *Hypopitys monotropa* Yellow Bird's-nest 
Crantz
#### Ecology
This inconspicuous myco-epiparasitic herb lacks chlorophyll and relies on Tricholoma fungi to extract nutrients from adjacent oak or beech trees. It grows in calcareous woodland where nutrients are extremely low.
#### Status & distribution
Scattered throughout the British Isles, but mostly in the southeast of England on the chalk. In Oxfordshire mostly in the Chilterns, but also in the Cotswolds and occasional on the Corallian Ridge.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) listed c. 20 sites of which eight were on the Cotswolds. Killick et al. (1998) recorded 20 tetrads, and in 2000-22 c. 13 localities in the county.
#### Conservaton work
None.

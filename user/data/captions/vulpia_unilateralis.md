### *Vulpia unilateralis* Mat-grass Fescue 
(L.) Stace
#### Ecology
This small annual fescue grows in dry, bare, well-drained, sometimes disturbed, ground such as thin chalk grassland, sandpits, railways and walls. It prefers slighly alkaline and very low nutrient soils.
#### Status & distribution
Scattered in southeast England, and scattered in Oxfordshire.
#### Trends
Often regarded as a neophyte since it was first reported in the British Isles in 1903, but Stace pointed out that its distribution is similar to that of Orchis simia. Killick et al. (1998) listed two sites - Bald hill and Beacon Hill,  on the top of the Chilterns scarp. Only ever recorded from 12 sites in Oxfordshire, during  2000-22 at four in the county and three in vc 23.
#### Conservaton work
None.

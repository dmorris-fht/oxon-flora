### *Ophrys insectifera* Fly Orchid 
L.
#### Ecology
A plant usually of slightly shaded lime-rich grassland, but also in alkaline bogs, notably on Angelsey, normally on substrate with very low nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
Scattered in England and western Ireland, but absent from Scotland,central Wales and southwest England. In Oxfordshire, almost exclusively on the Chilterns plateau.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) noted 17 localities and Killick et al. (1998) 15 tetrads, and in 2000-22 seven localities in the county.
#### Conservaton work
None.

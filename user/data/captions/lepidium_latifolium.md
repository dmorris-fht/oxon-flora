### *Lepidium latifolium* Dittander 
L.
#### Ecology
A tall rhizomatous perennial, preferring neutral pH and very high nutrients. Native in maritime grassland, saltmarshes and on sea walls. Formerly grown for flavouring.
#### Status & distribution
Widespread in the British Isles, but mostly in the northern Midlands and southeast of England. Rare in Oxfordshire.
#### Trends
In Oxfordshire first found in 1967 at Brize Norton, but not since. H J Killick saw it on the west bank of the Thames north of Sandford Lock (vc22) from 1991 until lost in 1998; one since 2000, in Oxford.

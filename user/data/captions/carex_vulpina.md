### *Carex vulpina* True Fox-sedge 
L.
#### Ecology
This is a large, tufted perennial of calcareous flood meadows, ditch edges and damp woodland, where it tolerates high nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
Scattered, mostly in southern England. Oxfordshire holds  a major population in the River Ray catchment.
#### Trends
Not recognized as a species in Druce's time; Killick et al. (1998) reported six localities, in 2000-22 11 have been reported; the increase probably reflects increased botanical activity in the River Ray area.
#### Conservaton work
The Rare Plants Group (now the Oxfordshire Flora Group) of the ANHSO has found that the single original plant at Asham Meads SSSI and BBOWT reserve flowers quite regularly and sets abundant seed, but declined in 2012. Colin Smith germinated some seed which was used to reinforce the population there with 38 new plants of which eight survived in 2011. It has been lost from two ditches at Fox Covert which became shaded, and flowered better after trees clearance in its woodland site. In 2007 seed from eight plants in Bucks and Oxon was collected by Aylesbury Vale District Council Biodiversity Team and BBOWT, and grown on by British Flora.  In 2008-11 1,100 plants were planted out by AVDCBT. Six sites, mainly BBOWT reserves, along the Ray in Bucks were used, with management varying from hay cut, and low intensity grazing, to streamside. Of these plants, 1,027 survived in 2011, but were not monitored in 2012. The Essex Farm plants were all lost by 2019. Seed was collected by the MSB at Fox Covert in 2019.

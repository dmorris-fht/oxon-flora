### *Orchis anthropophora* Man Orchid 
(L.) All.
#### Ecology
Restricted to calcareous and low nutrient grassland.
#### Status & distribution
Mainly in Kent and Sussex, scattered further north to Sheffield. Only ever one confirmed site in Oxfordshire, in the Chilterns, flourishing.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) mentioned several records which he doubted.  Killick et al. (1998) gave two sites.  Since 2000 only at one site.
#### Conservaton work
The single population known since 1936 on the scarp slope of the Chilterns is now hand-pollinated. Annual volunteer monitoring shows individual plants to be short-lived, with numbers fluctuating from 0 to 53. Sheep grazing has been discontinued, and the scrub is hand cut; fencing prevents deer and rabbit grazing. Horse grazing in the adjacent lower field to the north has resulted in plants colonizing the short sward there.

### *Anthemis cotula* Stinking Chamomile 
L.
#### Ecology
This is an annual to biennial of neutral and nutrient-rich arable land; it is absent from heavy clay soils.
#### Status & distribution
Mostly in central, eastern and southern England. Widespread and especially common on the Chilterns and Cotswolds, but rare on the southern Clay Vale.
#### Trends
Known to Druce (1927) as generally distributed and locally common. Killick et al. (1998  p. 249) had 187 tetrads in vc 23.  Crawley (2005) had records in the Vale of White Horse District, notably at  Kingston Down and Badbury. During 2000-22 known at 16 localities in Oxfordshire and 15 in vc 23.
#### Conservaton work
Introduced at the Warburg Nature Reserve, Bix.

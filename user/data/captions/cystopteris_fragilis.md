### *Cystopteris fragilis* Brittle Bladder-fern 
(L.) Bernh.
#### Ecology
This fern requires shady calcareous rocks or walls and tolerates slightly raised nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
Mostly in the north and west of the British Isles, it is near its southeastern limit in Oxfordshire where It was always rare.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) reported two sites. Killick et al. (1998) listed five tetrads, it was found at least twice at Taynton Quarries, but access has been limited recently. In 2000 no extant sites were known, however  it was found at All Saints, Faringdon in 2012 by M J Crawley and at Sandford St Martin by D M Morris in 2018.

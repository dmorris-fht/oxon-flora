### *Adonis annua* Pheasant's-eye 
L.
#### Ecology
This upright annual is usually found in autumn-sown cereals on dry calcareous soils of moderate nutrient status. It produces relativley few seeds which do not disperse far and have complex dormancy.
#### Status & distribution
Widely distributed but scattered in southern  England, and rare in northern England, Wales, Scotland and Ireland. In Oxfordshire widely distributed.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) listed seven localities, and Killick et al. (1998) had three tetrads in vc 23. Crawley (2005) reported two sites in vc22 in 1977, but none more recent. During 2000-2022 only at one site in the county.
#### Conservaton work
Threats include shading by the crop/weeds, lack of cultivation, and grazing by deer and rabbits. Unsown headlands with topping to control taller weeds is recommended. The Millennium Seed Bank, Kew, collected seed and carried out germination tests which found that it  requires a period of warmth followed by fluctuating temperature 20/10° C. An alternating temperature is a likely a depth sensing mechanism so that buried seeds do not germinate too deep in the soil (Chapman et al 2018) Trials by Plantlife  showed low establishment rates, especially on clay soils Shellswell et al. 2021

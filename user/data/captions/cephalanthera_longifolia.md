### *Cephalanthera longifolia* Narrow-leaved Helleborine 
(L.) Fritsch
#### Ecology
This long-lived perennial prefers open glades in calcareous woodland, but does not flourish under changing conditions such as coppice. C. longifolia has been found in Italy to be a mycorrhizal generalist with little colonization of the plant beyond the peripheral roots.
#### Status & distribution
It is curiously distributed being commonest along the western seaboard of Scotland.; it has suffered a severe decline in Wales and the New Forest, and is mostly absent from central and eastern England. Only known in Oxfordshire from Chinnor HIll/ Oakley Hill, and Sarsgrove Wood.
#### Trends
Druce and later recorders reported it only confirmed at Chinnor, last in 1970.

None.

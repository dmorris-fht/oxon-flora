### *Dactylorhiza praetermissa subsp. schoenophila* NA 
R.M.Bateman & Denholm
#### Ecology
This sub-species of the southern marsh orchid grows in neutral to base-rich flushes and fens with extremely low nutrients. It specialises in occupying calcareous fens dominated by Black Bog rush Schoenus nigricans.
#### Status & distribution
This taxon is mostly found in Norfolk, with a few other sites in southern England. In Oxfordshire it grows in two calcareous fens, which is a rare habitat. Numbers are not high in either location but a bumper year in 2009 saw 24 flowering spikes at one site.
#### Trends
Difficulties of identification have obscured the distribution of this subspecies.
#### Conservaton work
Both sites are receiving management and monitoring.
#### Variation
These slender, narrow-leaved, marsh orchids were known as Dactylorhiza traunsteineroides, partly based on observation of specimens collected from Cothill in 1945 by H.W Pugsley, but later they were named ‘Pugsley’s Marsh orchid’. In recent years other orchid experts have suggested that the slender marsh orchid plants present at the Ruskin Reserve and at Parsonage Moor nearby are a subspecies of the southern marsh orchid Dactylorhiza praetermissa and should be named Dactylorhiza praetermissa ssp. schoenophila (Bateman and Denholm, 2012), a sub-species of the southern marsh orchid that specialises in occupying calcareous fens dominated by black bog rush Schoenus nigricans.

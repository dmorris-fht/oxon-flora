### *Galium pumilum* Slender Bedstraw 
Murray
#### Ecology
This is a small perennial of dry, lime-rich and very low nutrient grassland. It seems to prefer longer sward where grazing by rabbits may be less severe.
#### Status & distribution
Restricted in the British Isles to southern and southeastern England. Scattered in Oxfordshire, which is at the northern edge of its range.
#### Trends
Always rare, Druce (1927) reported three sites and Killick et al. (1998) four tetrads. During 2000-22 only at two sites.
#### Conservaton work
The Flora Guardian is monitoring the populations and planning to send seed to the MSB. There is one population in cultivation.

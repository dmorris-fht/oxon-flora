### *Viola tricolor subsp. tricolor* Trilliw 
L.
#### Ecology
This is an annual or perennial which has suffered a big decline. On light, sandy soils with moderate nutrients; it was formerly in arable, but is now a plant of disturbed waste ground.
#### Status & distribution
Throughout the British Isles except at high altitude.  In Oxfordshire Killick et al. (1998) reported it mainly in the Cotswolds, but its distribution since 2000 has changed and it is mostly on the Corallian Ridge and the clay vales, absent from the Wessex Downs and very rare in the Cotswold and Chiltern Hills.
#### Trends
It has suffered a big decline. The distinction between this and V. arvense is not clear in Druce (1927). Killick et al. (1998) recorded 78 tetrads. During 2000-22 at c. 37 localities.
#### Conservaton work
None.

### *Orchis purpurea* Lady Orchid 
Huds.
#### Ecology
This tuberous orchid usually grows in basic to slightly acidic, low nutrient grassland.
#### Status & distribution
Resticted to southern England, mostly in Kent, with an outlier in Wales. I Oxfordshire estricted to the Chilterns with an outlier on the Wessex Downs.
#### Trends
Druce had no records, It has been known since 1961 at a semi-shaded site in the Chilterns. After a big national decline, mostly due to habitat loss, it is now increasing in Oxfordshire. It appeared at Hartslock SSSI BBOWT reserve, first flowering in 1999 (Chris Raper pers. comm.). DNA studies indicated an origin in the south of France, though it is unknown whether it blew in or was brought. Later a hybrid with the closely related Orchis simia was also found by Chris Raper (Bateman 2010). A single large specimen flowered at Odstone Lane in 2004, but only for two years (S E Erskine pers comm.).
#### Conservaton work
Seed from the confidential site was scattered at the Warburg BBOWT reserve in 1997 and plants have been flowering for three years.

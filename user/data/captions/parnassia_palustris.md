### *Parnassia palustris* Grass-of-Parnassus 
L.
#### Ecology
This small perennial requires short, unshaded sward and low nutrients. Spring-fed sites are often resilient due to phosphate being adsorbed by the tufa (Wheeler 2001).
#### Status & distribution
Widespread in the north and west of the British Isles, but restricted to  lime-rich soils in southern England. In Oxfordshire on the Corallian Ridge and the north of the county.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) quoted M Lobel as saying it was “plentiful around Oxford” in the 17th Century. This species was reported by Druce from 22 sites, and by Killick et al. (1998) from two in vc 23, only one of which is now extant. In 2000-22 two in Oxfordshire.
#### Conservaton work
Oxfordshire is an outlier to the south of its UK range, where populations differ in being diploid rather than tetraploid (Wentworth and Gornall, 1996). Extensive management is being carried out on it's sites at Lye Valley SSSI and Parsonage Moor SAC.
#### Variation
The varieties of Parnassia palustris L. in the British Isles (var. palustris and var. condensata Travis & Wheldon) each have two cytodemes: a diploid (2n = 18) and a tetraploid (2n = 36). The distribution of the cytodemes is loosely correlated with the limits of the Devensian glaciation.

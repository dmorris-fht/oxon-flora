### *Filago pyramidata* Broad-leaved Cudweed 
L.
#### Ecology
This small annual of dry, open ground prefers alkaline soils with moderate nutrients. Formerly an arable weed, it is a poor competitor, and does not respond well to nutrient enrichment or autumn sowing of cereal (Shellswell, 2021).
#### Status & distribution
In the British Isles it is scattered on the chalk in south east England. In Oxfordshire mostly on the Corallian Ridge and Chilterns plateau.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) reported eight sites in vc 23, and Bowen (1968) and Crawley (2005) reported seven in vc 22 Oxfordshire. Since 2000 only two sites have been extant - Sonning Chalk Pit, and last seen in 2014 at Buckland Warren SSSI, a now defunct golf course. Introduced at Aston Rowant NNR.
#### Conservaton work
Broad-leaved cudweeed is on Plantlife's 'Back from the Brink' programme; the Oxfordshire Flora Group has been monitoring the populations since 1997. Reintroduction by seed failed at Dry Sandford Pit in 2000. Dense grass was removed from the quarry floor at Sonning in 2021. Judy Webb proposed a small area within Compartment 7.3 of Aston Rowant National Nature Reserve for the reintroduction of Broad-leaved Cudweed. She gained agreement from Natural England, the landowner, and she and Cath Shellswell of Plantlife  wrote a reintroduction plan. Cath organised a grow-out from Kew’s Millennium seedbank which will provide the seed needed for the reintroduction. The grow-out to be funded through the Back from the Brink Colour in the Margins project. There is a photo of the young seedlings taken on 20/05/21 at the end of the reintroduction plan.

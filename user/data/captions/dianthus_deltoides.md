### *Dianthus deltoides* Maiden Pink 
L.
#### Ecology
This delightful small perennial is found in short sward, mainly, though not exclusively, on base-rich but very low nutrient, soils, overlying limestone or chalk.
#### Status & distribution
It is found scattered in the British Isles, except very rare in Ireland. In Oxfordshire it has a tiny population on the Corallian Ridge.
#### Trends
Bowen (1968) listed three sites in vc 22 Oxon of which only one survives. Druce listed Oxford plantings and Killick et al. (1998) gave Oakley Hill, Chinnor by R S R Fitter.
#### Conservaton work
Material sourced from Long Stow by B J Laney is now in cultivation at Frilford Bothy Vineyard. J A Webb has some seed of the Frilford Heath Golf course population and some plants grown from this. Waiting to re-introduce and bolster population when management of the area can be improved. Abington Green Gym volunteers grew plants from seed but they flowered finished and all died before they could be planted out. It is now spreading in the volunteers garden from seed - it  seems it can be a very short lived perennial once it has flowered.

### *Stellaria palustris* Marsh Stitchwort 
Ehrh. ex Hoffm.
#### Ecology
This is a blueish-green perennial, its slender stems, as Druce says, “preferring the company of other herbage”. It grows in marshy vegetation and wet MG4, favouring neutral soils with moderate nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
Scattered throughout the British Isles, but mostly in East Anglia. Widely distributed in Oxfordshire.
#### Trends
Nationally it has suffered a large decline. Druce (1927) reported 25 localities and Killick et al. (1998) had 36 tetrads, and in 2000-20022  there were c.17 sites reported in the county and vc 23.
#### Conservaton work
Introduced by the Freshwater Habitats Trust at Pinkhill, Farmoor (source Fowls Pill) in 2017. Not seen again for three years. Reappeared July 2021, present in August 2022. c. 20 plants.

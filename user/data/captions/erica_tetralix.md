### *Erica tetralix* Cross-leaved Heath 
L.
#### Ecology
A low-growing dwarf shrub of damp, open heathland. It requires highly acidic and extremely low nutrient soils.
#### Status & distribution
Throughout the British Isles except central south England. In Oxfordshire on the Cotswolds and the Chilterns.
#### Trends
Of Druce's two sites, not refound at Tadmarton, and last seen at Binfield in 2010 now overgrown with Rhododendron; and doubtful at Kingwood Common in 2004.
#### Conservaton work
At Binfield small non-flowering plants survived heavy grazing and trampling by deer when protected by logs and brash. Over-shading and grazing represent severe threats to this small colony and remedial action may be difficult. Efforts to interest the landowners have not been successful and the tiny population, last seen in 2010, is likely now lost. Introduced in 2021 at Matthew Arnold Field, Boars HIll.

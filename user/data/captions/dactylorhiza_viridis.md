### *Dactylorhiza viridis* Frog Orchid 
(L.) R.M.Bateman, Pridgeon & M.W.Chase
#### Ecology
This diminutive orchid favours slightly calcareous, very nutrient-poor, damp pasture, but is also found on moss humps in fens on Angelsey and on the slopes of the Chilterns and Wessex Downs.
#### Status & distribution
Common in northern Scotland and northern Ireland, this orchid is confined to the north, the Peak district, and to the chalk in southern England. In Oxfordshire on the Chilterns scarp, the Berkshire Downs and also damp hay meadows in the River Ray catchment.
#### Trends
While Druce (1927) reported 24 localities in vc23, Killick et al. (1998) had only nine tetrads. Eight sites have been reported duing 2000-22 on chalk downs and also one on Wendlebury Meads in the River Ray catchment.
#### Conservaton work
All its sites are on SSSIs which are being suitably managed.

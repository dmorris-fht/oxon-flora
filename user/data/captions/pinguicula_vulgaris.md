### *Pinguicula vulgaris* Common Butterwort 
L.
#### Ecology
This diminutive insectivorous perennial herb survives in wet, open, nutrient poor and neutral to acidic communities, usually on peat.
#### Status & distribution
Abundant in Scotland, western Wales and Ireland, and northern England and scattered in southern England. In Oxfordshire widely distributed but rare.
#### Trends
Druce knew it from 10 sites, and Killick et al. (1998) at five tetrads. In 2000-22 at three sites in Oxfordshire and one site in vc23.
#### Conservaton work
The plant reappeared at Lye Valley SSSI following scrub control by volunteers of the Friends of Lye Valley. Grazing by Highland cattle, and more recently, ponies, at Parsonage Moor keeps the sward shorter but breaks it up. The habitat is also threatened by very high nitrate use on the adjacent maize fields, which has resulted in an abundance of nitrophilous species in the SAC. A Wetland Environment Grant has enabled the Freshwater Habitats Trust to undertake scrub clearance from its former sties at Weston and Spartum Fens. At the Frilford site the sward in the area where it grows is being kept short and scrub removed.

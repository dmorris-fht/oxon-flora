### *Mentha pulegium* Pennyroyal 
L.
#### Ecology
A low-growing perennial of grazed damp, slightly acidic, grassland, flourishing with raised nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
Widespread in the British Isles, but mostly in the south. In Oxfordshire mainly on the lime-rich soils.
#### Trends
Sibthorp listed three sites - Binsey, Port Meadow and Otmoor and Druce reported six sites but noted "almost extinct". Killick at al. (1998) indicated that all extant sites were garden throwouts. S E Erskine found it probably  introduced with topsoil at Shellingford quarry where it has spread. Roger Heath-Brown says "I saw the pennyroyal on 6th July 2020, on a visit to the Frilford Golf Club with Fay Banks, who's a member. My records say "a dozen or so plants", "SU42799821, at the top of the bank of a man-made pond, behind one of the greens. The pond is well established and must be decades old. I presume it must have been introduced, but a fair time back."
It seemed quite happy there. David Morris said that he suspected that geese may spread it, "and though there were no geese when we visited Fay thinks she may have seen them there in the past. Anyway it looked just the sort of bank where there could have been waterfowl of some sort."
#### Conservaton work
None.

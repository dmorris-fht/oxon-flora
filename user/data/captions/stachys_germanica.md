### *Stachys germanica* Downy Woundwort 
L.
#### Ecology
Marren (1999) says this handsome biennial “is a poor competitor, requiring light and open conditions for germination and the first-year rosette phase of its life-cycle, and hence benefits from periodic woodland clearance by coppicing, scrub removal or hedge thinning. Its sudden appearances after a long absence suggest that the plant has a strategy of long-term seed dormancy, flowering and setting seed only in suitable conditions. The seeds are relatively large and heavy (one observer likened dehiscence to "coals falling from a scuttle"), and the main seed bank probably lies close to extant plants. Populations are extremely local with a characteristic clumped form. Records suggest that it may persist at suitable sites for well over a century."
#### Status & distribution
Only ever rare between Bristol, Lincolnshire and Kent. In Oxfordshire on the Cotswolds.
#### Trends
It has been restricted to the oolite of Oxfordshire for about 50 years (Dunn 1987). Recently at only four sites all in vc 23.
#### Conservaton work
When A J (Jo) Dunn "retired" the Cotswold Rare Plants Group  (now the Wychwood Flora Group) took up the tasks of monitoring, scrub clearance and scarification. In 2020 CR Lambrick raised plants from seed originally collected from the extinct (coniferized) site (Reed Hill ex SSSI) between Fawler and Stonesfield SP382172 in the 1980s and grown in gardens by John M Campbell Biological Records Oxfordshire County Council. About 45 plants were obtained, half of which were kept to flower for seed collection. Five plants were introduced at each of three sites in the Gloucestershire part of the AONB and seed was scattered at the Roll Right stones.

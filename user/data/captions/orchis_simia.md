### *Orchis simia* Monkey Orchid 
Lam.
#### Ecology
This orchid requires south facing grassland which is highly alkaline and very low in nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
Mainly in Kent and along the Chilterns with an outlier on Spurn Head.
#### Trends
Druce says the Caversham site is likely to be the first known in the country, observed by Mr Browne in c.1660, reported by John Ray in his catalogue of plants. It was considered absent from the Caversham area in 1853.  There are sporadic records at c. five other sites between 1880 and 1980. Known at  its current site since perhaps L. Darwall's record in 1836.
#### Conservaton work
The site is managed by BBOWT for this and other orchids by scrub clearance, sheep grazing and some chicken-wire guards.

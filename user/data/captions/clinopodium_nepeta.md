### *Clinopodium nepeta* Lesser Calamint 
(L.) Kuntze
#### Ecology
This is a short-lived perennial of  very highly calcareous, moderately nutrient-rich, dry, sandy or gravelly south-facing grassland, roadsides and railway embankments.
#### Status & distribution
In the British Isles mostly confined to Essex. Scattered in Oxfordshire.
#### Trends
While Druce (1927) listed 10 sites, it survived only  at Abbey Fishponds LNR, Abingdon (Killick at al. 1998). More recently it has been found at two new sites, both in vc 23.
#### Conservaton work
None.

### *Gagea lutea* Yellow Star-of-Bethlehem 
(L.) Ker Gawl.
#### Ecology
This early-flowering perennial is an Ancient Woodland Indicator which prefers moist, slightly raised nutrients and somewhat calcareous conditions.
#### Status & distribution
This  species grows scattered through England, Scotland and eastern Wales, but is commonest in the north of England. In Oxfordshire mostly in the north of the county.
#### Trends
While it has been increasing nationally, with us it has decreased from c. 20 localities listed by Druce (1927) to seven by Killick et al. (1998) and also during 2000-22. It may be under-recorded, as it often fails to flower in deep shade.  Very persistant and often looked for being an early flowering and delightful plant.

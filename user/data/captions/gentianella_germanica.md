### *Gentianella germanica* Chiltern Gentian 
(Willd.) Börner
#### Ecology
This striking gentian prefers highly alkaline and very low nutrient grasslands.
#### Status & distribution
Largely confined to the Chilterns, its curious absence from chalk grassland further south may be because it first established in the Chilterns, possibly as an introduction from its stronghold in eastern Europe. In Oxfordshire moslty on the Chilterns escarpment, but also on the Wessex Downs.
#### Trends
It appears to be spreading westwards (BSBI News 2009), and northwards. While Druce knew five sites, Killick et al. (1998) listed 24 tetrads; and 2000-22 about 13 loclities were recorded in the county.
#### Conservaton work
A montoring survey was made in 2009 of the BBOWT sites with records of this species.
#### Variation
It hybridises with G. amarella, and intermediates can be seen at the Warburg Reserve and Oakley Hill, and formerly at Crowell and Chinnor Hills (Killick et al. 1998) and Segsbury (Crawley 2005). However at Watlington Hill both species occur close together without intermediates. At this site the Chiltern Gentian grows in slightly taller sward, perhaps on deeper soil. Oakley Hill may be a Druce locality of the hybrid, which he named G. x pamplinii. If sheep grazing declines, the sward usually becomes too dense for this species which then survives along footpaths.

### *Lolium temulentum* Darnel 
L.
#### Ecology
A poisonous and formerly persistent annual grass of arable crops, it probably declined through better seed cleaning, and by the 1920s occurred mostly from seed for bird feeding. It prefers alkaline soils and favours high nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
Formerly widespread in England; since 1950 mostly a casual of waysides and tips. Widespread in Oxfordshire.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) had six sites and Killick et al. (1998) seven tetrads; in 2000-22 only one record at Blenheim Farm, BBOWT reserve, Charlbury.

None.

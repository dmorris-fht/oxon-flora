### *Dipsacus pilosus* Small Teasel 
L.
#### Ecology
This  bristly biennial is found on disturbed ground in woodland rides and edges. It requires damp conditions: ditches and stream banks, preferring highly calcareous and high nutrient soils.
#### Status & distribution
In the British Isles it is mostly found in the south Midlands, the southeast, and the Welsh borders, where the population is relatively stable. Scattered in Oxfordshire.
#### Trends
While Druce (1927)  listed ten sites in vc23, Killick et al. (1998) had eight tetrads, and during 2000-2022 known at six localities in Oxfordshire and four in vc 23.
#### Conservaton work
It requires  disturbance of the ground to germinate, and lack of woodland management may account for its decline.

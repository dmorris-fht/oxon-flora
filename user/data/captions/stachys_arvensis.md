### *Stachys arvensis* Field Woundwort 
(L.) L.
#### Ecology
This is an annual species of arable fields, allotments and road verges. It is generally found on calcareous to weakly acidic soils with slightly raised nutrients. It has a short-lived seed bank, and this may have contributed to its decline along with scrub invasion and afforestation.
#### Status & distribution
Mostly a plant of England, from Lincolnshire to Hampshire and Kent, and Wales, and usually coastal in Ireland and Scotland. In Oxfordshire mostly on the lime-rich soils of the Cotswolds, Corallian Ridge and Chilterns plateau.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) "in corn fields almost everywhere about Oxford + many others". Killick et al. (1998) listed 82 tetrads, but in 2000-22 only 17 localities all in vc 23.
#### Conservaton work
At City Farm a suite of arable annuals are being preserved.

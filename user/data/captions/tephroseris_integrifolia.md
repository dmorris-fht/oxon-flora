### *Tephroseris integrifolia* Field Fleawort 
(L.) Holub
#### Ecology
This is a biennial to short lived perennial preferring tightly grazed sward overlying chalk.
#### Status & distribution
This subspecies is found mostly on the chalk downlands of central southern England with outliers on Angelsey and in the north of England. In Oxfordshire mainly on the Wessex Downs but also in the Chilters, the Corallian Ridge and on the Cotswolds.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) reported 11 sites in vc23 on the Cotswolds and Chilterns, and 12 localities have been recorded on the Wessex Downs. Chalk downland has declined along with the decline of sheep grazing, probably accounting for the decline in this species both nationally and locally. In 2000-22 only at Aston Upthorpe and Whitehorse Hill SSSIs, Seven Barrows BBOWT NR and one unspecified locality, and one reported sighting at Aston Rowant NNR.
#### Conservaton work
Plantlife has made substantial scrapes at Aston Upthorpe in order to benefit Juniper, and this may help Tephroseris as well.

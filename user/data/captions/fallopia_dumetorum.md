### *Fallopia dumetorum* Copse-bindweed 
(L.) Holub
#### Ecology
This is a climbing annual of thickets and woodland edges which prefers well-drained, slightly acidic soils with high nutrients. It is self-pollinated and has a long-lived seed-bank and flourishes following disturbance.
#### Status & distribution
In the British Isles F. dumetorum is confined to southeast England. In Oxfordshire it is at the northern limit of its distribution and was scattered.
#### Trends
Killick et al. (1998) reported 12 tetrads in vc 23, but none have been refound. Since 2000 only in vc 22 at Tubney Wood, where Druce knew it (he said it is not in vc23).
Sibthorp (1794) p.130 Polygonum fagopyrum, Buck-Wheat Snake-Weed, corn fields, Comb [sic]
Druce says this is Buckwheat (Fagopyrum esculentum) for which he listed 12 sites besides Comb Sibthorp. John Ray says buckwheat was planted rarely.

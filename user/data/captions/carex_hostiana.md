### *Carex hostiana* Tawny Sedge 
DC.
#### Ecology
Preferring sedge-rich sites on mildly acid, but base-rich, and very low nutrient, fens and marshes, Killick et al. (1998) reoported it in eight tetrads, in five of which it has been re-found.
#### Status & distribution
In Britain mainly in the North and West, fewer in south and central England. In Oxfordshire mostly on the Corallian ridge,
#### Trends
Lost from many fen sites which have become overgrown. Druce (1927) listed eight sites (as C. fulva), and in eight tetrads in Killick et al. (1998) five of which were re-found. In 2000-22 at ten sites in the county and eight in vc 23.
#### Conservaton work
Volunteer work at the Ruskin Reserve and Cothill SAC has doubtless helped the species, and the Freshwater Habitats Trust's work at other fens will have made the sites more suitable. Plants grown on from seed from the south unit of Lye Valley were used to establish a population in the north unit.

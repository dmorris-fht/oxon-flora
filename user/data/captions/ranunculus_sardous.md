### *Ranunculus sardous* Hairy Buttercup 
Crantz
#### Ecology
This is an annual which flourishes on heavy clay soils, mostly in coastal pastures and disturbed ground. It prefers neutral pH and raised nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
In the British Isles mainly southern and eastern.  Scattered in Oxfordshire.
#### Trends
Druce "Very rare and probably extinct if it ever existed in the county." Killick et al. (1998) not reported. Found in 2003 on an organic farm, by S  E Erskine and S Gimson whilst conducting an arable flowers survey for the Northmoor Trust (Now the Earth Trust). Thereafter it was seen on biennial visits till 2011.

### *Ranunculus hederaceus* Ivy-leaved Crowfoot 
L.
#### Ecology
This is a small annual or perennial with creeping stems. It grows in open, muddy drawdown zones of ponds, streams and poached gateways. It prefers slightly acidic conditions, and slightly raised nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
Widespread in the British Isles and commonest in the west and north (except the northern Highlands).  Scattered in the county, but mainly on the Corallian Ridge.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) listed 23 sites in vc 23 and 29 in vc 22. Killick et al.  (1998) had four tetrads, Bowen (1968) said the species was absent from vc 22 Oxon, and Crawley (2005) said that 25 of Druce's 29 sites were no longer suitable. Flouishing only on Boars HIll.
#### Conservaton work
The Berkley Old Golf Course was formerly cattle grazed, and the western and central flushes both had open, poached, muddy areas with thriving populations of Ivy-leaved crowfoot. Now the area is mostly sheep-grazed; and rushes (Juncus spp.) invaded the western flush. The Oxford Preservation Trust employed the Abingdon Green Gym volunteers to dig out the rushes in 2021-2 and the Ivy-leaved Crowfoot flourished, only to have the area covered over by Creeping Bent-grass. Further work on the flush in April 2023  cleared some of the grass and created bunds across the stream. The rushes were cut back with a rotary mower.

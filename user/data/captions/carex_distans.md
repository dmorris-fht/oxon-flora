### *Carex distans* Distant Sedge 
L.
#### Ecology
In marshes, wet meadows and fens; mainly on mineral-rich soils which are slightly alkaline and nutrient rich. This species has long-lived seed, so may appear following disturbance.
#### Status & distribution
Carex distans has a curious distribution in the British Isles, being common around the coasts and in a broad swathe from the Severn to the Wash. In Oxfordshire it is widely distributed except on the chalk and limestone.
#### Trends
Nationally it has declined inland.  Druce (1927) listed five sites In vc 23 and Killick et al. (1998) mapped 54 tetrads, but these doubtless included misidentifications. During 2000-22 at 16 native sites, of which 10 were in vc 23.
#### Conservaton work
It depends less on managed sites than C. hostiana. Introduced at Milham Ford, Oxford.

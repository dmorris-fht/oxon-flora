### *Nymphoides peltata* Fringed Water-lily 
Kuntze
#### Ecology
This plant, formerly of slow-flowing rivers, prefers slightly calcareous and high nutrient conditions. Often introduced to ponds as an ornamental.
#### Status & distribution
Widespread, especially in England and Wales. Scattered in Oxfordshire.
#### Trends
This species has been lost from most of Druce’s 12 stations on the Thames, though found at Iffley Lock in 2001. Among 12 tetrads recorded by Killick et al. (1998) most of those in the Thames and Cherwell were not re-found post 2000. However it has increased markedly in ponds, probably introduced, it has at least 14 sites in Oxfordshire.
#### Conservaton work
Often released into water-bodies as an attractive ornamental, but perhaps not surviving long. Crawley (2005) infers from the presence of both pin and thrum flowers that the plant is native in our area.

### *Lysimachia tenella* Bog Pimpernel 
L.
#### Ecology
This diminutive evergreen perennial has creeping stems from which the flowers rise on short stalks. It needs wet open sites to survive, and prefers neutral soils with low nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
Mostly a plant of the western and southern seaboards of the British Isles. In Oxfordshire once widespread, now surviving almost exclusively on the Corallian ridge.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) had 16 sites in vc 23 and Killick et al. (1998) 15 tetrads. During 2000-22 found in seven localities in Oxfordshire and four in vc 23.
#### Conservaton work
At Frilford it dominates some stream banks with short mown turf. At Parsonage Moor, Lye Valley and Sidlings Copse volunteer work and grazing are now restoring the necessary open environment. The Freshwater Habitats Trust has an ongoing project to restore open habitats in several other valley fens (eg Spartum Fen where it used to grow).

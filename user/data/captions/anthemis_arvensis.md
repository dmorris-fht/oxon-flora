### *Anthemis arvensis* Corn Chamomile 
L.
#### Ecology
This is an aromatic annual of light calcareous or sandy soils, it grows mainly on arable land with moderate nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
It used to be common in the Midlands and southern England but scattered elsewhere. Widespread in Oxfordshire.
#### Trends
Like many arable flowers there has been a huge decline nationally and in Oxfordshire, due to improved seed cleaning and the use of herbicides.  Druce (1927) noted 34 localities in vc 23. After discussion with Q O N Kay and R C Palmer, many records submitted for the Flora of Oxfordshire were omitted, and only those for SP3818, 3820 and 5006 between 1970 and 1991 were accepted; two by H J M Bowen were found later. There was a further 63% decline between 1987 and 2003 (Braithwaite et al. 2006).It was regularly at Carswell Golf Course, though in small numbers. At Frilford South field it probably came in grass seed and spread briefly to North field in 2004. Since 2000 six sites have been reported in the county and three in vc 23..
#### Conservaton work
Some recent records are from seed mixes.

### *Colchicum autumnale* Meadow Saffron 
L.
#### Ecology
This is an Ancient Woodland Indicator that prefers grassy rides and lawns in slightly alkaline oak woodland, but also grows in damp meadows and tolerates moderate nutrients. Once destroyed because of its poisnous effects on stockA
#### Status & distribution
Now largely confined to its stronghold in the Cotswolds, the southern Welsh Marches, Somerset and Wiltshire. In Oxfordshire mostly in the Cotswolds with some on the Corallian Ridge, and very few eleswhere.
#### Trends
Known to Druce (1927) at 48 sites and by Killick et al. 1998 at 35, and at 15 during 2000-22.  Being highly poisonous to stock it is often destroyed, though frequently naturalized in gardens and used medicinally.
#### Conservaton work
None.

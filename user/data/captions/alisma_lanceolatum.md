### *Alisma lanceolatum* Narrow-leaved Water-plantain 
With.
#### Ecology
This is an emergent perennial herb of shallow water and exposed mud at the edges of ponds and canals.  It usually occurs in alkaline and high nutrient conditions.
#### Status & distribution
Widespread in central and southern England. Scattered in Oxfordshire.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) listed nine localities and there are 16 tetrad records in Killick et al. (1998), six north of Oxford from some of which it has not been recently recorded, notably the Farmoor reservoir area. There are nine 2000-22  records in the county and four in vc 23..
#### Conservaton work
It is abundant in two of the RSPB restored arable fields on Otmoor, suggesting longlived seed, and/or a requirement for open conditions.

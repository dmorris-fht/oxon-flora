### *Himantoglossum hircinum* Lizard Orchid 
(L.) Spreng.
#### Ecology
This dramatic orchid prefers very alkaline and extremely low nutrient grasslands.
#### Status & distribution
Scattered in southeast England and rare further west and north.
#### Trends
Not recorded in Oxfordshire till 1920 close to a plant in Headington that had been introduced from France (Druce  1927).  Altogether seen in a total of eleven sites in the county. During 2000-22 only at one longstanding and two new roadside sites.
#### Conservaton work
On its road verge site it is protected by wooden posts and has had a copper collar in one year. It has suvived for seven years and increased from one to two flowering spikes. At its BBOWT site it was fenced in, but the vegetation within the fence became very dense and the plant has not been reported since 2010.

### *Bromus interruptus* Interrupted Brome 
(Hack.) Druce
#### Ecology
This annual of arable fields was most frequently found on calcareous soils.
#### Status & distribution
Of unknown origin this species appeared in 1849 in Bedfordshire, spread widely and then declined.
#### Trends
It reached Oxfordshire in 1888, increasing to ten sites by 1927, but was last seen in 1931 at Taynton (OXF).
#### Conservaton work
Seed collected in Cambridgeshire was bulked up by the Millennium Seed Bank and sown by English Nature into ex-arable at Aston Rowant NNR (and in Cambridgeshire). More than 100 plants set seed in 2005; but due to difficult weather, ploughing was late and only tens were present the following year; also the vegetation became more grassy and less open. the plant was not found after 2007.In 2007 sheep got in, and ate all the young seed heads. Ploughing and monitoring in subsequent years failed to reveal any B. interruptus, corroborating reports that the plant is very palatable and the seed has limited dormancy (Graham Steven pes. com.).

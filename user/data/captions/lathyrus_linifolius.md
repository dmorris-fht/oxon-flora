### *Lathyrus linifolius* Bitter-vetch 
(Reichard) Bässler
#### Ecology
Restricted to moist, rather acid soils with moderate nutrients, growing in open, and also in slightly shaded ancient woodland sites. It persists well in suitable habitats but appears to be a poor coloniser. In Oxfordshire it has two distinct habitats. It is somewhat light demanding, occurring in damp heathy grasslands, and in open woodlands and on wood edges on ancient woodland sites.
#### Status & distribution
Well distributed in western and northern Britain but rather rare in the midland and eastern counties of England. In Oxfordshire mostly on the Chilterns plateau, but also in damp meadows in the west.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) listed 20 sites, but Killick et al. (1998) only 11 tetrads. In 2000-22 only at five sites.
#### Conservaton work
At one woodland site in the Chilterns it grows on a steep, sandy bank and responded well to extra light following tree clearance.

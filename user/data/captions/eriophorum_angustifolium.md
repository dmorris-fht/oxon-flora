### *Eriophorum angustifolium* Common Cottongrass 
Honck.
#### Ecology
This a perennial of wet acid grassland and marshes with extremely low nutrient levels.
#### Status & distribution
Plentiful in upland Britain; it is declining in the lowlands due to loss of habitat as wetland becomes drained and is fertilised. In Oxfordshire it grows in the calcareous fens of the Corallian Ridge and and a wet hay meadow by the River Windrush, but is probably lost from the River Ray catchment.
#### Trends
In Druce’s floras it was described as local in 1886, but by 1927 it was only at 18 sites and decreasing. Killick et al. (1998) noted it as being lost from most of its sites, only extant at five, and five were known during 2000-22.
#### Conservaton work
Intense conservation work is ongoing at its five sites including one SAC, two SSSIs and two LWSs.

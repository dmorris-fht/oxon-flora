### *Helosciadium repens* Creeping Marshwort 
(Jacq.) W.D.J.Koch
#### Ecology
A very small creeping umbellifer of the drawdown zone of rivers and other water bodies, preferring sunny, slightly alkaline and high nutrient conditions. It is slightly self-fertile and has long-lived seeds.
#### Status & distribution
Always very rare in England, Wales and Scotland, now also known from Norfolk and Essex. Formerly at four localities in Oxfordshire, along the rivers Thames and Windrush, which declined to one by the 1990s.
#### Trends
Lost from Witney and Chilswell.  By 1990 in the British Isles only  known on Port Meadow and Binsey Green. In Oxfordshire last seen at Binsey Green in 2005, now only native on Port Meadow.
#### Conservaton work
English Nature’s Species Recovery Programme supported monitoring, introductions, basic experiments and a visit to European sites by the Rare Plants Group of the Ashmolean Natural History Society  of Oxfordshire (McDonald & Lambrick, 2006). NE continues to support monitoring and introductions. Now introduced and flourishing at North Hinksey, Cutteslowe and Henley. Unsuccessful introductions - two along the Cherwell near Marston. For  a full discussion see Stroh et al. 2016 https://bsbi.org/wp-content/uploads/dlm_uploads/Apium_repens_species_account.pdf
#### Variation
A hybrid with H. nodiflorum also occurs on Port Meadow

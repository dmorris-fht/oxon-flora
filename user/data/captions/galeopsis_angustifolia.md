### *Galeopsis angustifolia* Red Hemp-nettle 
Ehrh. ex Hoffm.
#### Ecology
This annual grows on calcareous soils in moderate nutrient conditions, and is also a plant of screes. In Oxfordshire it is now seen mostly in quarries, gravel pits and on conservation headlands.
#### Status & distribution
Widespread in England, Wales and Ireland, mainly in the south. Widespread in Oxfordshire.
#### Trends
It had a massive decline, perhaps partly because being late-flowering, it fails to set seed in winter-sown crops. Druce (1927) reported it as common, and Killick et al. (1998) listed 57 tetrads in vc 23. During 2000-22 it was recorded at seven localities in the county and six in vc23.
#### Conservaton work
Its main population is on an arable headland which is ploughed for a suite of arable weeds.

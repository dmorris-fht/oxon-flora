### *Orchis militaris* Military Orchid 
L.
#### Ecology
This tuberous perennial prefers highly calcareous grassland or scrub, with very low nitrogen levels.
#### Status & distribution
Moslty restricted to southeast England, in Oxfordshire in the Chiltern hills with one record in West Oxfordshire and one on the Corallian ridge,  by J Bobart in 1719.
#### Trends
While Druce listed 11 sites, it was considered extinct in the country after 1931 until refound in 1947 in Buckinghamshire. At  Eversdown BBOWT Reserve it ws found in 1984 and 1986, this site appears still suitable despite some scrub growth. Also reported  in tetrads 7484 and 7292, but only briefly. Newly found in West Oxfordshire in 2013 but not seen since.
#### Conservaton work
The last site, Eversdown BBOWT Reserve, appeared suitable despite some scrub growth. Plants raised in Sweden from seed from the Chilterns was introduced at the Warburg Reserve, Bix, in 1996.

### *Onobrychis viciifolia* Sainfoin 
Scop.
#### Ecology
This is a small to medium-sized perennial, decumbent to erect, which grows on unimproved dry calcareous grassland with low nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
Common in central and southern England, but rare in Scotland, Wales and Ireland. In Oxfordshire mostly on the Cotswolds and in the north of the county, but also on the Chilterns, the Corallian Ridge and to a lesser extent on the Wessex Downs.
#### Trends
Druce said it was "Common and looking native on the chalk, but I think only the relic of cultivation." Killick et al. noted it was "A former forage crop now little grown but long and thoroughly naturalized in dry pastures, rough ground and waysides. Recorded at over 50 sites during 2000-22.
#### Conservaton work
None.
#### Variation
It was used as a fodder crop and is now extensively found in S E Erskined mixes used on road verges, embankments and cuttings. Its natural distribution is therefore obscured, but native plants are usually smaller than those used for fodder or derived from them. It is assumed to be native in old grassland such as found in the Chilterns, Wessex Downs and in North Oxfordshire.

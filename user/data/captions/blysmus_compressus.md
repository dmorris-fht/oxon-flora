### *Blysmus compressus* Flat-sedge 
(L.) Panz. ex Link
#### Ecology
This inconspicuous, rhizomatous monocot grows in damp calcareous and low-nutrient grassland, mostly in wet flushes.  It is tolerant of low salt levels - Druce (1927) and Bowen (1968) recorded it at the Marcham Salt Spring LWS,
#### Status & distribution
Widespread in England, but only common in the north. In Oxfordshire, uncommon but widespread.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) listed five sites, Bowen (1968) three in vc 22,  and Killick et al. (1998) four tetrads. Refound near Marston (Almonds Farm) in 2007, probably where it was known to Druce in the 1880s. Only one site in Oxfordshire and vc 23 in 2000-22.
#### Conservaton work
The Almonds Farm LWS flush is scythed to maintain a short sward. Twelve small clumps from Almonds Farm were introduced in 2018 to a mown area at the Oxford Golf Course as near the site of Druce's locality - Bullngdon Green.  In cultivation at Milham Ford Park. Propagated in the Freshwater Habitats Trust's GroWet project. It could be considered for re-introduction to its historic site at Marcham Salt Spring LWS.

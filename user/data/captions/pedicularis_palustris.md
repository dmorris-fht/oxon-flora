### *Pedicularis palustris* Marsh Lousewort 
L.
#### Ecology
This is an annual or biennial hemi-parasite, which grows in fens with neutral pH and very low nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
Mostly in the northern and western parts of the British Isles, with strongholds also in the New Forest and Norfolk. Scattered in Oxfordshire.
#### Trends
Druce (1886) "The meadows near Moulsford and near Hurley I have seen quite crimson from the profusion of this semi-parasitic plant, which I have found attached by suckers (haustoria) to the roots of Carex acutiformis, C. riparia, and Juncus articulatus." In 1927 Druce reported 26 sites, Bowen (1968) seven in vc 22 Oxon, but Killick et al. (1998) reported only seven, in 2000-22 reduced to two native sites in vc23 and one (Cothill SAC) in Vale of White Horse (vc22 Oxon).
#### Conservaton work
The parasitic nature of Pedicularis palustris makes it a useful ecosystem engineer for controling large graminoides (Decleer et al. 2013). Introduced (source Lye Valley)  at Simon's Land, River Mead, Hinksey Heights Fen, Chilswell Valley,  Matthew Arnold's Field Fen, Boars Hill. and Pinkhill, Farmoor - now spreading to many new areas across this site. Many 100s of plants in 2022.

### *Carex pulicaris* Flea Sedge 
L.
#### Ecology
This very small sedge grows in mineral-rich, but very nutrient-poor, neutral soils in damp meadows and fens.
#### Status & distribution
It is widespread in the British Isles,  Scattered in Oxfordshire.
#### Trends
It has declined sharply in the south-eastern Midlands. In Oxfordshire it has been lost from several fens due to scubbing over, but re-appeared at Lye Valley.
#### Conservaton work
Work by Wild Oxford and the Lye Valley volunteers has restored open conditions there, though the water supply is enriched. Natural England is working to maintain open condiions at the Cothill NNR  and BBOWT at the adjacent Parsonage Moor and Lashford Lane SSSIs. The Freshwater Habitats Trust project has restored suitable open conditions at  Spartum Fen and Barrow Farm Fen SSSIs (both former sites).

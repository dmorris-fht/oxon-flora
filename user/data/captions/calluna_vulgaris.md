### *Calluna vulgaris* Heather 
(L.) Hull
#### Ecology
Heather is a shrubby perennial of very acidic and very low nutrient soils. It declines when shaded and in wet conditions.
#### Status & distribution
Widespread in the British Isles. In Oxfordshire moslty on the Chilterns plateau, with some on the Corallian Ridge and a little on the Cotswolds.
#### Trends
Recorded by Druce (1927) at 36 sites and by Killick et al. (1998) at 40 tetrads.  While most of Druce's records were in the west and central parts of the county, Killick's were mostly on the Chilterns clay-with-flints. During 2000-22 at c. 30 in the county with c. 27 in vc 23.
#### Conservaton work
At Sydlings Copse SSSI and BBOWT reserve heathy areas were cleared of bracken and scrub without success. At Shotover SSSI cuttings were propagated from existing plants, and planted out successfully. At Frilford Golf Course SSSI areas of rough were scarified and sown with harvesting from Surrey. At Cumnor Hurst SSSI areas have been cleared of scrub and heather is hanging on.

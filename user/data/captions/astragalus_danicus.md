### *Astragalus danicus* Purple Milk-vetch 
Retz.
#### Ecology
This low-growing perennial is found in calcareous grassland with very low nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
A. danicus is curiously distributed, being coastal in the north-east of the UK, and inland on limestone and chalk in the south. In the lime-rish areas of Oxfordshire - the Cotswolds, Chilterns, Wessex Downs and the Corallian Ridge.
#### Trends
It has always been rare in the county with four sites in Druce (1927) and five tetrads in Killick et al. (1998),  none confirmed.  Not refound at Taynton Down verge, now only known at Westwell Gorse.
#### Conservaton work
The BBOWT reserve Westwell Gorse is grazed, but the roadside site, though monitored and tended by the Wychwood Flora Group, became too dense.

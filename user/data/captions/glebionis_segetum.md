### *Glebionis segetum* Corn Marigold 
(L.) Fourr.
#### Ecology
This conspicuous annual weed of arable fields and wasteland germinates in spring and prefers light soils which are low in lime and have slightly raised nutrient levels.
#### Status & distribution
Once common throughout England but mostly coastal in Scotland, Ireland and Wales. Throughout Oxfordshire but moslty in the north and on the Chilterns plateau.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) listed 33 sites while Killick et al. (1998) had 186 tetrads, and during 2000-22 only 18 localities most of which were wildflower seed-mixes.
#### Conservaton work
Now a frequent component of wildflower mixes used on conservation headlands.

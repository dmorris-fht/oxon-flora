### *Genista tinctoria* Dyer's Greenweed 
L.
#### Ecology
This small leguminous shrub is characteristic of old meadows and rough pastures. It grows on calcareous and neutral to slightly acidic, heavy soils with extremely low nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
Widely distributed in England and Wales. In Oxfordshire half the sites are in hay meadows in the River Ray catchment, and the others scattered on the chalk and the Corallian Ridge.
#### Trends
Reported by Druce (1927) at 19 sites, and by Killick et al. (1998) at 18 tetrads in vc23; during 2000-22 at c. 14 localities in the county with a large population on Otmoor.
#### Conservaton work
As it is larval foodplant of the  Greenweed Flat-body Moth Agonopterix atomella, part of the population at Otmoor is being left uncut each year.

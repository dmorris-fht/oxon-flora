### *Scleranthus annuus* Annual Knawel 
L.
#### Ecology
A deep rooted annual which stands out in dry conditions; preferring neutral and moderately nutrient-rich soils.
#### Status & distribution
Scattered throughout England Wales and Scotland, rare in Ireland. Widespread in Oxfordshire, but mostly on the Chilterns plateau.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) described it as "locally common on arable land with dry sandy soils"; Killick et al. (1998 p.116) reported it in only 23 tetrads in vc23,  and  many of those were in the south, and have not been found recently, despite extensive searches made for arable flowers in the late 1990s. Now only known at a new site near Eynsham.

### *Carex lepidocarpa* Long-stalked Yellow-sedge 
Tausch
#### Ecology
This sedge favours damp, alkaline, base-rich, seasonally flooded fens with very  low nutrient conditions.
#### Status & distribution
In Britain mainly in the north and west. Scattered in Oxfordshire but absent from the Wessex Downs.
#### Trends
Its decrease in southern England is probably due to modern farming and drainage, and increase in nitrate deposition. Druce (1927) listed ten sites and Killick et al. (1998) ten tetrads. During 2000-22 at 11 sites in Oxfordshire and seven in vc 23.
#### Conservaton work
Natural England and the Freshwater Habitats Trust are working on a number of alkaline fens to develop open short sward.

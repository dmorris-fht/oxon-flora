### *Oenanthe fistulosa* Tubular Water-dropwort 
L.
#### Ecology
This perennial is characteristic of floodplain hay-meadows (MG4), but is also found in pasture and along water margins, on alkaline substrate often with high nutrients. At Bestmoor it occurs as the only common forb in a floodplain meadow, suggesting it may be herbicide resistant.
#### Status & distribution
Scattered throughout the British Isles except Scotland, the north of England and the south west. In Oxfordshire mostly adjacent to the Rivers Ray, Cherwell and Thames.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) reported about 30 sites, and Killick et al. (1998) 58 tetrads, an increase probably reflecting more recording in the Ray area. In 2000-22 records indicate c. 58 localities, a large majority of which are on designated sites.
#### Conservaton work
Most of the sites lie in the Upper Thames Tributaries ESA, and appropriate management of MG4 was eligible for payments under HLS since meadows with this plant often qualify as UKBAP priority habitat Lowland Meadows. Introduced by the Fresh Water Habitats Trust to PinkhIll Meadow at Farmoor (Thames Water) (source Fowls Pill, Otmoor) in 2017. Present every year up to last survey in August 2022. Slowly spreading. 100s of plants in 2022

### *Pilularia globulifera* Pillwort 
L.
#### Ecology
This is a peculiar, small, rhizomatous fern that grows around the edges of water (usually ponds) in open conditions. It prefers a substrate that is acidic and very low in nutrients.
#### Status & distribution
This is a lowland plant growing mostly in the south and west. It has a patchy distribution and was always uncommon. The only Oxfordshire site was on the Chilterns plateau.
#### Trends
First recorded in the pond at Cane End by Vera Paul, and  refound in 1970, but not seen by R S R Fitter in 1974. Pillwort has declined nationally, probably due to fewer pond margins being open mud with very low nutrients.
#### Conservaton work
Introduced at Nettlebed Common in 2017, still there in 2018.

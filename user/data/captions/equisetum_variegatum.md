### *Equisetum variegatum* Variegated Horsetail 
Schleich. ex F.Weber & D.Mohr
#### Ecology
In calcareous fens, at the edge of clear shallow water, among lime-encrusted mosses and short vegetation; it is highly dependent on scrub clearance and grazing. In infertile places including dune-slacks, river shingle and loch shores.
#### Status & distribution
It has a patchy distribution in the British Isles, and is absent from southeast England. In Oxfordshire only ever known at one site on the Corallian Ridge. Dry Sandford Pit is a recent sand working, so the spores were perhaps blown in  from the nearest site 100 km away in south Wales (or it survived formerly in the adjacent fen sites).
#### Trends

#### Conservaton work
In 2009 768 shoots, 38 with cones, were found, but in 2023 only c.50.  The area has been cleared of scrub, but is now choked by Carex disticha and Phragmites communis.

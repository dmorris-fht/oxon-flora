### *Anacamptis morio* Green-winged Orchid 
(L.) R.M.Bateman, Pridgeon & M.W.Chase
#### Ecology
This tuberous perennial grows where grass is not too dense, and nutrient levels are low. It is usually found on base-rich soils, and occurs in both well-drained conditions and on heavy clays. In Oxfordshire it is mostly in hay meadows and on road verges, but also churchyards, undisturbed pasture, and quarries.  Populations tend to fluctuate.
#### Status & distribution
Widespread in England especially in the south. In Oxfordshire mostly in the north of the county, and espcially in the Ray catchment.
#### Trends
Druce (1927) listed 39 sites in vc 23, and Killick et al. (1998) had 44 tetrads, some almost certainly now lost; and in 2000-22 recorded c. 31 localities in the county.

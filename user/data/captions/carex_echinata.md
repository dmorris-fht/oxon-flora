### *Carex echinata* Star Sedge 
Murray
#### Ecology
In a variety of marshy and boggy sites which are at least seasonally waterlogged. Prefers very acidic and very low nutrient conditions.
#### Status & distribution
Common in most of the British Isles except the southern Midlands. In Oxfordshire widespread but rare.
#### Trends
Lost from Druce (1927) and Sibthorp's 15 sites, and from most of the eight sites named by Killick et al. (1998), and also from seven sites in vc22 Oxon.  This is consistent with the usual decline of small acid-loving species (Walker 2006). Reported at Alvescot (in 2007-17), but not refound more recently (this site is not strongly acidic).
#### Conservaton work
Introduced at Nettlebed Claypits in 2017.
